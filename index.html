    • ORIGAMI

    Inspired by Japanese paper craft, the Origami is a functional yet aesthetic piece of brewing equipment for the right coffee connoisseur.

    It stands out for its flexibility in containing both cone and wave paper filters for those who enjoy experimentation in flavour profiles.

    HISTORY

    Handcrafted from the Mino region of Gifu prefecture in Japan, known for their exceptional ceramic craftmanship.

    Each dripper is carved with 20 vertical grooves that pass air along to bring out the best flavor of the beans.

    The dripper's ceramic build also provides high thermal conductivity and heat retention, ensuring even coffee extraction throughout the process.

    PROFILE

    Taste Notes: Extracts rich, sweeter, fuller coffee than Hario V60. Produces a well-balanced acidity profile which maintained as the coffee cooled.
    Material: Porcelain
    Colours: 15 colours available (ranging from pastel to bright colours). (See colour options here.)
    Paper type: Cone or Wave filter papers

    Coffee Lab Project

    By: Nicole Koh

    This is my first HTML project, inspired by my love for coffee brewing equipment.

    I wanted to build a website where beginners can find all the relavant information they need to pick their first filter coffee dripper (As the market is oversaturated with too many options). The website is divided into the following parts: Summary, History, Profile, Recipe, Purchasing links, and Suggestions for other methods.

    The profile section describes the expected taste notes from using this brewing method, its material construction, available colours, and the type of filter paper it is compatible with. Furthermore, the recipe section provides detailed instructions and a video tutorial to guide beginners on using the product. Purchasing Links are also provided as I remembered how confusing it was to find resellers that sold authentic versions of the product in my country. Lastly, I provided suggestions to other drippers so users can compare between different products before deciding which dripper is most suitable for their taste preferences, budget, and convenience.

    I enjoyed learning how to incorporate Bootstrap functions such as the carousel slideshow and cards to streamline the display of images. Addtionally, I tried to experiment with some hovering animations to add some depth into the elements. Embedding youtube videos turned out eaiser than expected. Finally, I learnt how to convert my logo into a favicon icon for the image next to the tab title of the page.
